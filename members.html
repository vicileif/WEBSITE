Prof. Dr. Johanna Löchner

Prof. Löchner works at the Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen–Nuremberg in the Department of Psychology. Her research focuses on the early detection and prevention of mental disorders in children and adolescents using digital technologies. In addition, she is interested in risk and resilience factors in the development of mental disorders, as well as stress management—for example, constructive ways of dealing with negative emotions. Based on this work, Prof. Löchner develops digital applications such as apps and mobile assessment tools within multidisciplinary teams, aiming to capture symptoms in everyday life and provide low-threshold prevention of mental health problems.

Dr. Severin Hennemann

Dr. Hennemann is a licensed psychological psychotherapist in cognitive-behavioral therapy. Hennemann works at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz in the Department of Clinical Psychology, Psychotherapy and Experimental Psychopathology. His research focuses on digital health, including internet-based cognitive behavioral therapy (iCBT), chatbots, and factors influencing the acceptance and adoption of e-mental-health interventions. Further interests include ambulatory assessment, digital diagnostics, and psychotherapy process research. For his research, he received the Early Career Award of the International Society of Behavioral Medicine (ISBM) in 2023 and the Young Investigator Award of the European Association of Psychosomatic Medicine (EAPM) in 2022.

Prof. Dr. Björn Schuller

Dr. Schuller is affiliated with the Technical University of Munich (Medicine & Health), Imperial College London (Computing), audEERING GmbH, the Harbin Institute of Technology, and Tianjin Normal University. He works in the fields of artificial intelligence, machine and deep learning, and audio, language, bio, and multimedia signal processing. His research spans health informatics, digital health, mHealth, and affective computing, with additional interests in computer ethics.

Dr. Lasse B. Sander

Dr. Sander is currently working as a group leader at the Institute of Medical Psychology and Medical Sociology at the Medical Faculty of the University of Freiburg, Germany.
