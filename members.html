Prof. Dr. Johanna Löchner

Prof. Löchner works at the Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen–Nuremberg in the Department of Psychology. Her research focuses on the early detection and prevention of mental disorders in children and adolescents using digital technologies. In addition, she is interested in risk and resilience factors in the development of mental disorders, as well as stress management—for example, constructive ways of dealing with negative emotions. Based on this work, Prof. Löchner develops digital applications such as apps and mobile assessment tools within multidisciplinary teams, aiming to capture symptoms in everyday life and provide low-threshold prevention of mental health problems.

Dr. Severin Hennemann

Dr. Hennemann is a licensed psychological psychotherapist in cognitive-behavioral therapy. Hennemann works at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz in the Department of Clinical Psychology, Psychotherapy and Experimental Psychopathology. His research focuses on digital health, including internet-based cognitive behavioral therapy (iCBT), chatbots, and factors influencing the acceptance and adoption of e-mental-health interventions. Further interests include ambulatory assessment, digital diagnostics, and psychotherapy process research.
