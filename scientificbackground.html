Scientific Background

The MENTALLM⁽ᴿᴺ⁾ network brings together researchers from psychology, psychiatry, computer science, and digital health to advance the scientific understanding and application of large language models (LLMs) in mental-health research. Our work is grounded in evidence-based clinical science, computational modeling, and the methodological foundations of machine learning. Across our disciplines, we investigate how LLMs can support early detection, assessment, prevention, and intervention in mental health—while also critically examining their limitations, reliability, and ethical implications. By integrating expertise in psychodiagnostics, digital phenotyping, ambulatory assessment, human–AI interaction, and algorithmic transparency, we aim to develop robust, clinically meaningful, and ethically responsible AI-based tools. Our scientific background reflects a shared commitment to methodological rigor, open collaboration, and interdisciplinary innovation. This foundation enables us to evaluate emerging technologies, design new research paradigms, and contribute to the evidence base for safe and effective AI applications in mental-health science..
